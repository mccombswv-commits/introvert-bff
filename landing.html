The toolkit

Five tools.
One for every direction.

Most communication tools help you send things. This one helps you figure out what to send, whether to go, how to read what came in, and how to handle what happens when you get there.

Should I Even Go?

The Decision Engine

Answer five questions. Get a verdict — Go, Skip, or Negotiate — with actual reasoning, not guilt. Factors in your social battery, the event type, the relationship, and your gut feeling. Which, by the way, is almost always right.

I Need the Words

The Wordsmith

Decline gracefully, reach out awkwardly, exit a conversation, or craft the perfect non-committal response. AI-written, tailored to your relationship and tone. Refine until it sounds like you — but better.

Read It & Reply

The Interpreter

Paste any message. Find out what it really means — the subtext, the emotional undercurrent, what they're actually asking for. Then get three ready-to-send reply options in your tone. For the texts you've been staring at for an hour.

Help Me Survive This

The Survival Kit

Full event prep, conversation starters, exit lines, social battery check-ins, and an escape hatch when you're ready. From walking in to slipping out — you're not alone in there.

Get Me Out Now

The Emergency Extraction

The Big Red Button. Fake incoming call with a read-aloud script. Alarm trigger. Emergency fake text. The Slow Burn. The Medical. One tap and you're on your way out the door. Last resort. Zero judgment. Designed to be screenshot-worthy — because we know you'll send it to a friend.

The People Pleaser

"I said yes to three things this weekend because I didn't know how to say no without a 400-word explanation and two apologies."

→ I Need the Words writes the no. Gracefully. Warmly. Without the essay.

The Overthinker

"I've written this fourteen times. Version one was too casual. Version nine was too long. Version fourteen starts with 'Hey so' which is somehow worse than all the others."

→ I Need the Words generates it. Refine until it's exactly right. Send. Breathe.

The One Running on Empty

"I've been 'on' for five days straight. I have one thing left. I have nothing left. I need a plan for getting through it and getting home without being rude about it."
